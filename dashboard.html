The key insight: Ramp enforces card policy — spend limits, category restrictions, CFO thresholds. The control plane enforces procurement policy — approved vendor lists, hard dollar limits, ERP readiness rules. They run independently on the same transaction. txn_007 (Marriott $380) and txn_008 (Amazon Business $95) clear both gates: within card limit, approved category, approved vendor, Oracle Expenses-ready journal entry. Divergent outcomes (txn_001, 002, 005, 006) are not bugs — they are the architecture. The Controller sees both layers.
